1. Information Security Policies (A.5)
Organizations must have information security policies approved by management. These policies must be communicated to all employees and reviewed at planned intervals.

2. Organization of Information Security (A.6)
Roles and responsibilities must be clearly defined. Access to information must follow the principle of least privilege. Remote working and teleworking arrangements must follow documented security rules.

3. Human Resource Security (A.7)
Before employment: background checks may be required.
During employment: employees must receive security awareness training.
After employment: access rights must be removed immediately, and company assets returned.

4. Asset Management (A.8)
All information assets must be inventoried and classified (public, internal, confidential). Removable media must be controlled. Assets leaving the organization require authorization.

5. Access Control (A.9)
Access rights must follow business needs. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is recommended for critical systems. Passwords must follow the organization's password policy. Access reviews must occur regularly.

6. Cryptography (A.10)
Use industry-standard cryptographic controls. Encrypt data in transit and at rest. Key management practices must be documented.

7. Physical and Environmental Security (A.11)
Server rooms must be locked and monitored. CCTV may be used for sensitive areas. Clean desk policy must be implemented. Environmental risks (fire, flood) must be mitigated.

8. Operations Security (A.12)
Change management must be controlled. Anti-malware tools must be deployed. Logs must be collected and monitored. Development, test, and production must be separated.

9. Communications Security (A.13)
Networks must be segmented based on risk. Secure email gateways and VPNs must be used for remote access. Data transfer must be protected.

10. System Acquisition, Development, and Maintenance (A.14)
Security must be integrated into SDLC. Code reviews and security testing must be performed. Third-party components must be evaluated for risk.

11. Supplier Relationships (A.15)
Suppliers must follow security requirements. Contracts must include confidentiality and data protection clauses. Supplier access must be monitored.

12. Information Security Incident Management (A.16)
Organizations must have an incident response plan. Incidents must be reported immediately. Lessons learned must be documented.

13. Business Continuity Management (A.17)
Business continuity plans (BCP) and disaster recovery plans (DRP) must exist. Plans must be tested regularly.

14. Compliance (A.18)
Information processing must comply with laws and regulations. Records must be protected from unauthorized access and modification.

EXCLUDED INFORMATION
